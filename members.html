Founder

Alongside founding St Andrews Fashion Collective, Stephanie Irwin is a fashion blogger with an affinity for sportswear, editorial, and public relations. Beginning her foray into fashion as Style Editor of The Saint, Stephanie progressed through founding her blog, www.princessintrainers.com, Deputy Editing The Tribe and playing an integral role in the DON’T WALK PR team. Professionally, Stephanie has internship experience ranging from Research Analysis at Rosseau Asset Management to Business Development at Noel Asmar Equestrian, outfitter of the Canadian Olympic Equestrian teams. Through her roles, Stephanie aims to promote cohesive brand image and professionalism. The interests of Stephanie particularly reside within fashion E-commerce, fashion business journalism and learning from high-achieving peers. Check out Stephanie's blog Princess In Trainers

Tatiana Krotovskaya

Tatiana has interned at several PR and Branding agencies in fashion capitals such as London, New York and Moscow. Juno Lee

Juno Lee is a South Korean citizen who lives in Tokyo, Japan. Before coming to St Andrews, he spent 3 years in the East Coast of the United States, where he met his current business partner Pierre, Founder of Mocha Joe’s Roasting Company. Mocha Joe’s is a Specialty Coffee company that focuses on working with farmers to grow, harvest, sort and export organic and specialty coffees. Juno mainly focuses on expanding the Mocha Joe’s brand and its development, and the exporting business to new markets. Along with his Entrepreneurial ventures, he is also a singer-songwriter in Japan, where he first released his single album in 2014 with Japanese record companies, Lastrum Music and Juggler Company Limited. Currently, two of his music are featured in Japanese corporate commercial and a well-anticipated TV show. One of Juno’s fastest growing passions is Fashion. Growing up in cities like Tokyo and Seoul where the fashion scenes are unique and exhilarating, Juno is currently seeking entrepreneurial endeavors within the industry.

Rina Agboraw

As a British citizen of Cameroonian and Ghanaian descent, raised in Austria, Japan and Canada - Rina is passionate about fashion industry globalization, and is a champion for increased diversity within it.Rina’s career interests center around public relations, event planning, and journalism. However, she has recently gained interest in model management, styling and photography. Rina is constantly seeking opportunities to be creative and work with like-minded, hard-working and passionate individuals. Professionally, Rina has experience ranging from an internship at prestigious London-based label – ROKSANDA, to her current roles in both the St Andrews Africa Summit (SAASUM) and the St Andrews African Caribbean Society (STA – ACS).

Maya McDonald

Maya is a freelance professional stylist, currently working with socialite clients in Mumbai, London, and Kampala. For her clients, Maya purchases compelling, quality goods that reflect their values. Maya gained her love for high fashion after attending a Versace runway show at the tender age of five. Ever since, bold and bright fashion have become synonymous with her style.In addition to styling, Maya acted as the Executive Director of the The Inside Out Project St. Andrews, by world-renowned street artist, JR, which highlighted her skills in creative direction and model selection. The image shown here is her portrait from the Inside Out exhibit, with the word she chose being a Sanskrit word meaning, “a place beyond the beyond.” She looks forward for interning for Cartier this summer.
